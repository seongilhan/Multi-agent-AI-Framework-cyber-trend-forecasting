#### **2. Technology Stack and Integration**
##### **A. Kubernetes Security Hardening (OPA + Falco)**
- **Open Policy Agent (OPA)**:
  - **Purpose**: Enforce **RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)** and **admission policies** to prevent unauthorized CronJob/container injections.
  - **Implementation**:
    - Deploy **OPA Gatekeeper** as a **Kubernetes admission controller** to validate API requests.
    - Configure **policy-as-code** to block **privileged escalation** (e.g., `securityContext.capabilities.add: ["ALL"]`).
    - Integrate with **Falco** for runtime enforcement.
  - **Version**: OPA v0.40.0 (latest stable) + Gatekeeper v3.16.0.
  - **Configuration**:
    ```yaml
    # Example Gatekeeper policy to block privileged pods
    apiVersion: templates.gatekeeper.sh/v1beta1
    kind: ConstraintTemplate
    metadata:
      name: k8sprivileged-pod
    spec:
      crd:
        spec:
          names:
            kind: K8sPrivilegedPod
    ---
    apiVersion: constraints.gatekeeper.sh/v1beta1
    kind: K8sPrivilegedPod
    metadata:
      name: privileged-pod-blocker
    spec:
      match:
        kinds:
          - apiGroups: [""]
            kinds: ["Pod"]
      parameters:
        allowed: false
    ```

- **Falco**:
  - **Purpose**: Detect **container escapes, unauthorized binaries, and lateral movement**.
  - **Implementation**:
    - Deploy **Falco as a DaemonSet** with **Kubernetes audit logs** integration.
    - Configure **AI-driven anomaly detection** (Falco’s **ML-based rules**) to flag unknown payloads.
    - **Version**: Falco v0.37.0 (with **AI rule engine**).
  - **Key Rules**:
    ```yaml
    # Detect unauthorized container exec
    - rule: UnauthorizedContainerExec
      desc: Detect unauthorized exec into containers
      condition: container and execve and not user.name in group("kube-system")
      output: "Container exec detected (user=%user.name container=%container.name pod=%pod.name)"
      priority: WARNING
    ```

##### **B. Hybrid DDoS Protection (Scut + AI Rate-Limiting)**
- **Scut (Cloudflare Scrubbing Center)**:
  - **Purpose**: Mitigate **AI-augmented DDoS** via **edge-side protection**.
  - **Implementation**:
    - Deploy **Scut as a Kubernetes Ingress Controller** with **AI WAF**.
    - Configure **rate-limiting rules** to block **adaptive traffic patterns**.
    - **Version**: Scut v2.0 (with **AI-driven traffic analysis**).
  - **Configuration**:
    ```yaml
    # Example rate-limiting rule in Scut
    rate_limit:
      requests_per_second: 1000
      burst: 2000
      policy: drop
    ```

- **AI Rate-Limiting (Falco + Prometheus)**:
  - **Purpose**: Detect **abnormal traffic spikes** (e.g., **IoT-based amplification**).
  - **Implementation**:
    - Integrate **Falco with Prometheus** for **real-time metrics**.
    - Set **alert thresholds** for **unusual request volumes**.
    - **Version**: Falco v0.37.0 + Prometheus v2.46.0.

##### **C. Zero Trust Expansion (Prisma SASE)**
- **Prisma SASE (Palo Alto Networks)**:
  - **Purpose**: Enforce **mTLS for service-to-service communication**.
  - **Implementation**:
    - Deploy **Prisma SASE as a service mesh** (via **Istio integration**).
    - Enforce **least-privilege access** for **service accounts**.
    - **Version**: Prisma SASE v4.0 (with **mTLS enforcement**).
  - **Configuration**:
    ```yaml
    # Istio mTLS policy
    apiVersion: security.istio.io/v1beta1
    kind: PeerAuthentication
    metadata:
      name: default
    spec:
      mtls:
        mode: STRICT
    ```

##### **D. Automated Botnet Detection (CrowdStrike XDR)**
- **CrowdStrike XDR**:
  - **Purpose**: Detect **self-replicating botnet traffic**.
  - **Implementation**:
    - Deploy **CrowdStrike sensors** on **Kubernetes nodes**.
    - Integrate with **Falco for behavioral correlation**.
    - **Version**: CrowdStrike XDR v5.0 (with **AI-driven threat hunting**).
  - **Key Features**:
    - **Self-replicating process detection**.
    - **IoT-based traffic isolation**.

## **1. Asia-Pacific (APAC) Compliance Strategy**

### **Regulatory Requirements**
APAC presents unique challenges due to **data localization laws** and **strict cybersecurity regulations**. Key regulations include:

- **China’s Cybersecurity Law (2017)**: Mandates data localization for personal information and critical infrastructure. Organizations must store data within China’s borders, requiring adjustments to cloud-based solutions like **OPA and Falco** to ensure data residency compliance.
- **Singapore’s Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA)**: Requires organizations to implement **access controls, encryption, and regular audits** to protect personal data.
- **Japan’s Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI)**: Similar to GDPR, APPI enforces strict data protection measures, including **consent management and breach notification**.

### **Regional Threat Patterns**
APAC faces significant threats from **phishing, ransomware, and credential theft**, with **33% of intrusions** involving prior compromise. The region is also vulnerable to **advanced malware and reconnaissance tools** like **DRAGONJUICE** and **SODINOKIBI**.

### **Implementation Adjustments**
- **Data Localization**:
  - Deploy **OPA Gatekeeper** and **Falco** in **region-specific data centers** to comply with China’s Cybersecurity Law.
  - Use **encrypted Kubernetes clusters** to ensure data residency.
- **Zero Trust Expansion**:
  - Enhance **Prisma SASE** with **region-specific mTLS policies** to align with **Singapore’s PDPA** and **Japan’s APPI**.
  - Implement **AI-driven anomaly detection** in **Falco** to detect **SODINOKIBI-like malware**.
- **Incident Response**:
  - Establish **region-specific incident response teams** trained in **APAC threat patterns** (e.g., **phishing, ransomware**).
  - Ensure **72-hour breach notification** compliance with **PDPA and APPI**.
